State-of-the-Art Pipeline Inspection

Our Inline Inspection (ILI) services utilize cutting-edge smart pigging technology to provide comprehensive internal assessments of your pipeline infrastructure. We detect and characterize metal loss, cracks, geometry anomalies, and other defects with industry-leading accuracy.

Technologies

Inspection Technologies

Multiple advanced technologies to detect and characterize all types of pipeline anomalies.

Magnetic Flux Leakage (MFL)

High-resolution metal loss detection for general corrosion and pitting. Our MFL tools provide accurate wall thickness measurements and defect sizing with 90%+ accuracy.

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

High-accuracy wall thickness measurement and crack detection. UT technology provides precise metal loss quantification and is ideal for crack-like defect detection.

Electromagnetic Acoustic (EMAT)

Advanced crack detection and stress corrosion cracking assessment. EMAT technology excels at detecting axially oriented cracks and crack colonies.

Capabilities

What We Detect

Metal Loss

General corrosion, pitting corrosion, and erosion with accurate depth and extent measurements.

Cracks

Longitudinal, circumferential, and crack colonies including SCC and fatigue cracks.

Dents & Buckles

Mechanical damage, dents, wrinkles, and buckles with precise depth and strain analysis.

Geometry

Pipe diameter, ovality, wall thickness variations, and bend radius measurements.

Features

Valves, tees, flanges, welds, and other pipeline features with precise location data.

FAQ

Common Questions

What pipeline diameters can you inspect?

Our tools accommodate pipeline diameters from 4 inches to 56 inches. We have specialized tools for both small-diameter gathering lines and large-diameter transmission pipelines.

How accurate are your inspection results?

Our MFL tools achieve 90%+ depth accuracy for metal loss features. UT tools provide wall thickness measurements accurate to within ±0.1mm. All tools are regularly calibrated and qualified per API 1163.

What is the typical inspection speed?

Inspection speeds typically range from 1-5 m/s depending on product flow and tool technology. We optimize speed to ensure data quality while minimizing operational impact.

Do you provide dig verification support?

Yes, we offer comprehensive dig verification services including excavation supervision, NDT inspection, and detailed comparison reports between predicted and actual anomalies.
